About Us

As a co-ed cappella group at UCLA, AweChords strives not only to share its passion for music with the LA community collectively as a group but also to have its members realize the potential of their voices and musicality. Originally named “Nhip-Am”, it was formed in 2006 as a choir for the annual Vietnamese Culture Night. Since then, the group has expanded to more diverse audiences throughout Los Angeles and in 2010 regrouped to become AweChords.

While having its origins rooted in VCN, AweChords represents the ethnic, academic, and musical diversity in the UCLA student body through its own members. In the last several years, the group has included North and South campus majors, foreign exchange students from Korea and Taiwan, as well as members with varying musical backgrounds. In spite of this variety, all past and present members ultimately shared one mission: to learn, love, and live music and to bond with each other in the process. AweChords welcomes its future members and hopes to inspire them to pursue this mission.

To expand its musical horizons, AweChords has often explored its eclectic musical style by not only singing but also experimenting with the arrangement process. In turn, we've come to sing a variety of genres and artists from Justin Timberlake, Sara Bareilles, Coldplay, and One Republic, to some oldies such as Toto and even broadway shows such as Wicked.

AweChords' musical style continually evolves with each successive year. While the group has primarily worked with pop, indie, and some oldies, the group continues to actively work in expanding its repertoire to captivate its audience.
